Information about

Western Optical Transient Survey

Optical Sky Survey in

Northern British Columbia, Canada

Western Optical Transient Survey or WOTS is an optical sky survey started in August, 2020 in Northern British Columbia, Canada. The survey uses a 0.33-m telescope + CCD, which robotically takes images of the sky systematically. The survey was started by Professional Astronomer Malhar R. Kendurkar, who is the Principal Investigator and a regular user of the 1.83-m Plaskett Telescope at Dominion Astrophysical Observatory located on Observatory Hill, in Saanich, BC which is operated by National Research Council of Canada (NRC / CNRC).

The survey performs several science operations during it's run every clear night which includes Optical Transient Survey, Multi-Messenger Astronomy, Minor Planet Survey and Optical Follow Ups of Transients discovered by other sky surveys. WOTS is also a part of international collaboration between Astronomers and Amateur Astronomers to search and study optical transients using several robotic observatories called Global SuperNova Search Team.

WOTS had been extensively used in the past for the study of Eclipsing Binary Stars, gathering Times of Minima (ToM) of over 1500 stars in Northern Hemisphere, precise calculations of Radial Velocities and has resulted over 90 publications in peer-reviewed journals.
